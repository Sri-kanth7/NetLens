# 04 - User Personas

## Persona 1: Home Network Owner
**Profile**: A technically curious homeowner who wants to understand network health without logging into a router.

**Goals**
- Check whether the internet is healthy.
- See which devices are connected.
- Identify unusual usage or weak signal areas.
- Receive alerts when the network is down or unstable.

**Pain Points**
- Router dashboards are cluttered and hard to interpret.
- Device-level visibility is fragmented or too technical.
- There is no simple summary of what is happening now.

**How NetLens Helps**
- Provides a clean, premium dashboard.
- Highlights internet status, ping, devices, and alerts at a glance.
- Surfaces historical usage and network health trends.

## Persona 2: Gamer
**Profile**: A latency-sensitive user who cares about ping, packet loss, and stable connectivity.

**Goals**
- Monitor ping and packet loss in real time.
- Detect devices or activity causing spikes.
- Understand peak usage windows.

**Pain Points**
- Gaming performance degrades unpredictably.
- Router UI rarely explains performance issues well.

**How NetLens Helps**
- Surfaces live latency-related indicators.
- Makes bandwidth and device activity visible.
- Supports future AI insights for slow internet detection.

## Persona 3: Small Business Admin
**Profile**: An operations-focused user responsible for keeping staff connectivity stable.

**Goals**
- Monitor multiple devices and alert conditions.
- Understand whether the network is degrading.
- Share visibility with other team members later.

**Pain Points**
- Multiple users and devices make troubleshooting difficult.
- Need more than consumer-grade controls.

**How NetLens Helps**
- Prepares the architecture for organizations and multi-network management.
- Supports roles and permission boundaries.
- Creates a path toward reporting and auditability.

## Persona 4: School IT Coordinator
**Profile**: A support-oriented user managing network health across a larger environment.

**Goals**
- Track multiple networks and collectors in the future.
- Respond quickly to alerts.
- Monitor performance trends and device status.

**Pain Points**
- Needs scalable structure, not a home router interface.
- Requires role-based access and reporting later.

**How NetLens Helps**
- Aligns to multi-tenant architecture.
- Supports future collectors and network segmentation.
- Offers a foundation for enterprise-style permissions.

## Persona 5: Network Enthusiast
**Profile**: A power user who wants visibility into devices, uptime, signal, and behavior.

**Goals**
- Explore analytics, device states, and alerts in depth.
- Understand network performance over time.
- Compare usage across devices and networks.

**Pain Points**
- Existing tools are either too simple or too fragmented.

**How NetLens Helps**
- Combines operational clarity with strong visualization.
- Leaves room for AI, reports, and advanced analytics.
